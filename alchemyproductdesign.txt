Good. We’re out of prompt mode.

Here’s the **actual product design direction** for Alchemy Unbounded, built on the current site’s structure, voice, and offerings: the site already has a homepage promise, About, three service pillars, testimonials, pricing, contact, and a “Book a Free Discovery Call” entry point. The redesign should keep that foundation, but turn it into a tighter, more cinematic product experience. ([Alchemy Unbounded][1])

# Product definition

**Alchemy Unbounded helps thoughtful, inwardly searching people return to clarity, creativity, and conscious living without the pressure, noise, and generic energy of typical self-help websites.**

The current brand already promises deep transformation in life, work, creativity, and consciousness, and positions Harish across transformational life coaching, creativity coaching, and movement arts training. ([Alchemy Unbounded][1])

The new product should feel like this:

* not a coaching brochure
* not a spiritual sales funnel
* not a luxury wellness page

It should feel like a **quiet digital world you step into**.

# Final product structure

Keep the product to seven main routes:

* `/` Home
* `/about`
* `/the-work`
* `/offerings`
* `/journey`
* `/voices`
* `/begin`

This is a cleaner, more intentional version of the current public structure, which already centers Around About, service pages, testimonials, pricing, and contact. ([Alchemy Unbounded][1])

# 1. Final user journey

## Entry

**Emotion:** quiet discomfort, curiosity, fatigue
**User lands on:** Home
**What they see:** one emotional truth, one strong visual, very little noise
**What moves them forward:** recognition

## Recognition

**Emotion:** “this understands something I haven’t said out loud”
**User moves to:** About or The Work
**What they see:** the problem named clearly, then the guide introduced with humility

## Trust

**Emotion:** “this feels real, not performative”
**User moves to:** Offerings or Voices
**What they see:** Harish’s lived journey, real credentials, real client shifts. The current site already has strong material for all three. ([Alchemy Unbounded][2])

## Fit

**Emotion:** “there may be a path here for me”
**User moves to:** Offerings and Journey
**What they see:** not packages first, but pathways

## Readiness

**Emotion:** calm willingness
**User moves to:** Begin
**What they see:** one clear invitation, no pressure

## Action

**Emotion:** trust
**User action:** discovery call / contact

# 2. Homepage design

The homepage should work like a film.

## Scene 1: Opening frame

**Purpose:** immediate recognition
**Headline:**
**You are not broken.**
**You have simply drifted away from yourself.**

**Supporting copy:**
A life can look fine on the outside
and still feel quietly misaligned within.

**Layout:** centered, narrow text width, near-fullscreen
**Visual:** still portrait or slow atmospheric film clip, no montage
**Motion:** text fades in gently, no dramatic movement

## Scene 2: The pause

**Purpose:** slow the user down
**Copy:** one short line only

**Line:**
Most people never stop long enough to notice it.

**Layout:** large whitespace, no buttons
**Why:** this page must establish its pace early

## Scene 3: The discomfort

**Purpose:** name the real internal tension
**Headline:**
You kept going.
But something deeper stayed untouched.

**Body:**
You learned how to function.
How to perform.
How to keep moving.

But clarity did not come from movement alone.

**Layout:** left-aligned editorial block
**Visual:** none or faint texture
**Motion:** almost still

## Scene 4: The villain

**Purpose:** define the conditioned life
**Headline:**
A life can become efficient
without ever becoming fully alive.

**Body:**
Routine. Expectation. Performance.
Inherited roles. Unquestioned patterns.
A way of living that works, but never quite feels like yours.

**Layout:** slightly denser section, darker background
**Why here:** tension must rise before the shift

## Scene 5: The shift

**Purpose:** first relief
**Headline:**
The answer is not to become someone else.
It is to return.

**Body:**
To what is true.
To what is yours.
To the life beneath the noise.

**Layout:** wide open section, lighter background
**Motion:** soft reveal

## Scene 6: The guide

The current About page gives you the right raw material: Harish is presented through lived struggle, service, long inner work, formal training, and a move away from corporate life toward this work. ([Alchemy Unbounded][2])

**Headline:**
I’m Harish.
I’m not here to stand above your journey.
I’m here to walk with you through it.

**Body:**
This work comes from lived experience, deep inner work, years of study, and a life shaped by transformation from the inside out.

**Layout:** split screen, image left, copy right
**CTA:** Read the story

## Scene 7: The three paths

The current live site already groups the work into transformational life coaching, creativity coaching, and movement arts training. ([Alchemy Unbounded][1])

Use three editorial panels:

### Life

Return to clarity, alignment, and the life that feels like your own.

### Creativity

Clear the inner noise and reconnect with original expression.

### Body

Build a movement practice that restores intelligence, resilience, and presence.

## Scene 8: Proof

The live homepage and testimonials page already include multiple client stories across countries and professions. ([Alchemy Unbounded][1])

Do not use a carousel.

Use 3 fixed story blocks:

* before
* shift
* after

## Scene 9: Final invitation

**Headline:**
If something in you already knows,
begin there.

**CTA:** Begin with a conversation

No hard sell. No urgency tricks.

# 3. About page

The current About page is rich. It already contains Harish’s self-definition, the work, credentials, the personal journey through sorrow, anxiety, shame, emptiness, body transformation, and the larger vision. ([Alchemy Unbounded][2])

The problem is not lack of depth.
It is **too much density without enough pacing**.

## New structure

### Section 1: Who I am

Keep it intimate, but cut repetition.

**Headline:**
A lifelong student of life, creativity, and human transformation.

### Section 2: The work I do

Shorter, clearer

**Headline:**
I work with people who are ready to live with more clarity, depth, creativity, and conscious presence.

### Section 3: My journey

This is the emotional heart of the page.

Break it into four mini-scenes:

* the difficult early years
* the turn inward
* the body transformation
* leaving the old life behind

### Section 4: My training

The current credentials are strong and should stay, especially training with Eric Maisel, Jill Badonsky, Leon VanderPol, Stevie Kent, and movement practice rooted in Fighting Monkey. ([Alchemy Unbounded][2])

But present them as:

* teachers who shaped the work
* not a certification wall

### Section 5: The vision

End with the larger philosophy of Alchemy Unbounded

**Headline:**
A space for real transformation, where creativity, consciousness, and life are no longer separated.

### CTA

Explore the work

# 9. Design system

## Colors

* Deep Charcoal: `#191A1D`
* Bone: `#F5F2EB`
* Fog: `#E8E4DD`
* Warm Slate: `#736C63`
* Muted Amber: `#B98A4A`

Use amber rarely.
If it appears everywhere, it loses meaning.

## Typography

* Display: Canela / high-end serif equivalent
* Body: Inter / clean modern sans
* Accent italic: only for rare emotional lines

## Spacing

* 8px base grid
* 160px vertical section spacing desktop
* 96px tablet
* 72px mobile
* narrow text column for long-form reading

## Surfaces

* mostly flat
* almost no visible cards
* separators through space, not borders

---

# 10. Interaction and motion

## Motion rules

* text reveal: 220–320ms
* section reveal: 320–420ms
* page transition: subtle fade, 250ms
* hover states: minimal tint or underline shift

## Use motion for

* emotional pacing
* scene transitions
* revealing depth slowly

## Never use motion for

* decoration
* showing off
* constant parallax
* bouncing CTAs
* testimonial carousels

The site should move like breath returning.

# 13. What to remove from the current feel

The current site’s strength is sincerity and emotional depth. Its weakness is that the depth sometimes arrives in long, uninterrupted stretches, which makes the experience feel more like reading a heartfelt document than moving through a directed product. ([Alchemy Unbounded][1])

So remove:

* long uninterrupted text blocks
* repeated emotional phrasing
* service-page sprawl
* too many equal-weight sections
* proof that arrives without narrative framing

Keep:

* Harish’s honesty
* the philosophical depth
* the three-path structure
* the real testimonials
* the non-salesy tone

# 14. The real design principle

**The product should feel like it is guiding the user inward, not pulling them forward.**

That is the whole game.
